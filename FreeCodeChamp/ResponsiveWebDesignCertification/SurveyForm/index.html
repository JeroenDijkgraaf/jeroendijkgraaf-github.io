Biography

  • 1886 - Born in in the small village of Starzeddel (Starosiedle), Province of Brandenburg, which was then part of Germany
  • 1898 - Tillich was sent to Königsberg in der Neumark (now Chojna, Poland) to begin his gymnasium schooling
  • 1900 - Tillich's father was transferred to Berlin, resulting in Tillich's switching in 1901 to a Berlin school, from which he graduated in 1904
  • 1904 - Starts his university career
  • 1911 - He received his Doctor of Philosophy degree at the University of Breslau
  • 1912 - Receives his Licentiate of Theology degree at Halle-Wittenberg and is ordained as a Lutheran minister in the Province of Brandenburg
  • 1914 - In October he joined the Imperial German Army as a chaplain during World War I; burying his closest friend and numerous soldiers in the mud of France
  • 1925 - Served as a Professor of Theology at the University of Marburg, where he began to develop his systematic theology
  • 1933 - When Adolf Hitler became German Chancellor in 1933, Tillich was dismissed from his position
  • 1934 - Tillich moved with his family to the United States. Started teaching at Union Theological Seminary in New York, where he began as a Visiting Professor of Philosophy of Religion
  • 1951 - Publishes his Systematic Theology in which he examines the inner tensions in the structure of reason and being, primarily through a study in ontology
  • 1954 - Is appointed at Harvard Divinity School, where he was University Professor
  • 1965 - Tillich died on October 22, 1965, ten days after having a heart attack
